Why Users Join (The Incentive Strategy)
Our theory of public user motivation — discussed in our sessions
The core insight: Scouts are not just users — they're the intelligence network. Their incentive to participate is what powers the whole platform. Get this wrong and there's no data. Get it right and growth is exponential.
Layer 1: Utility Value (The Practical Reason)
Before they contribute anything, the app must give immediate value: "I can find out where to go tonight, right now." Even with just 10 venues and basic data, the app solves a real problem every Friday night. First-time users don't need to rate anything — they just need to see a vibe score that saves them from showing up to a dead venue.
Layer 2: Status & Clout (The Social Reason)
Clout points, scout tiers (Newbie → Elite), leaderboards, accuracy scores, achievement badges — these create a reputation system. In Lagos social culture, being the person who "knows where the party is" is real social capital. Being an "Elite Scout" is a flex. The leaderboard makes it competitive. People want to be ranked #1 at their favourite venue.
Layer 3: Community (The Belonging Reason)
Crew system, Cartel Radar, squad locations, Vibe Persona archetypes — these create belonging. "My crew is all Turn Up personas" or "The Luxe Cartel runs Shiro on Saturdays." People join communities, not apps. The social graph creates lock-in that raw utility never can.
Solving the Cold Start Problem
The chicken-and-egg problem: No scouts = no data. No data = no reason to join as a scout.

Our solution:
1. Demo mode — app looks fully functional with seeded data from day one
2. Recruit 200 "Elite Scouts" first — influencers, promoters, photographers (they have intrinsic motivation: their audience)
3. Pay scouts for the first 3 months — small rewards (airtime, merch, cash prizes for leaderboard winners) until organic motivation kicks in
4. Venue partnerships — venues incentivize their own customers to rate ("Rate us for free drinks before 11pm")
5. WhatsApp groups — coordinate scout activity for high-density data on specific nights
The Long-Term Flywheel
More scouts → more ratings → better vibe scores → more users come for the data → app gets known → more influencers want Elite Scout status → venues promote the app → venues send their own customers to rate → more ratings… The flywheel self-perpetuates once it starts spinning. The challenge is getting it spinning the first time — that's what the launch night strategy solves.
